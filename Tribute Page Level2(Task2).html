Introduction

Sudha Murthy is an Indian author, social worker, and philanthropist.

She is the chairperson of the Infosys Foundation and known for her contributions to education and social welfare.

Her books, written in English and Kannada, often depict her real-life experiences and focus on social issues.

Sudha Murthy is admired for her simplicity, humility, and dedication to improving the lives of others.

She has inspired millions through her work and continues to be a guiding light for many.

Services

Sudha Murthy's services include her role as the chairperson of the Infosys Foundation, which works towards education, healthcare, rural development, and more.

She has initiated numerous projects aimed at providing quality education to underprivileged children and supporting rural development.

Through the foundation, she has also contributed significantly to healthcare, particularly in the areas of healthcare infrastructure and medical research.

Her services extend beyond India, as she actively participates in international philanthropic efforts to promote education and social welfare.

Sudha Murthy's dedication to service has earned her widespread respect and admiration.

Life

Born on August 19, 1950, in Shiggaon, Karnataka, Sudha Murthy grew up with a passion for literature and social work.

She pursued higher education in engineering and later joined the Tata Group as the first female engineer.

Sudha Murthy's life took a significant turn when she married N.R. Narayana Murthy, the co-founder of Infosys, in 1978.

Together, they founded the Infosys Foundation in 1996, which has since become one of the most prominent philanthropic organizations in India.

Throughout her life, Sudha Murthy has remained committed to her values and principles, making a positive impact on society.
